Mental Health Awarness

What is Mental Health?

Mental health is the emotional, psychological, and social well-being of a person.

This can usually affect a person's ability to enjoy life. Factors that can affect it are stress, depression, anxiety, etc.

Around 46% of people meet the criteria for a condition thats diagnosible. Half of those people develop conditions around the young age of 14.

The condition most people are affected by is Anxiety, in America having 42.5% of adults affected.

How can I support someone in this crisis?

Bringing up a certain topic you know someone is struggling about is definitely not an easy task. In order to ask someone about anything they're struggling with, you must be mentally prepared yourself and take a calm approach.

Set time aside and make sure there are no distractions that might surround you. The conversation you should have with the person should be open and non-judgemental. You should listen carefully to what they have to say and let them share as little or much as they'd like to.

Make sure you understand your limits on the support you can provide.

What are signs of this crisis?

Some signs of this crisis may include feeling restless, having difficulties sleeping, trouble concentrating etc.

Symptons like these alone cannot predict someone is mentally struggling, but are able to indicate if something is wrong.

Illnesses can develop in young children as well usually through their behavior that may include changes in their school performance, frequent tantrums, nightmares, agression, etc.
